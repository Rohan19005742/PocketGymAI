# AI Coach - User Guide & Example Conversations

## Overview

The AI Coach is your personal fitness expert, powered by 75+ research papers covering strength training, nutrition, recovery, and injury prevention. It provides:

- Evidence-based workout programming
- Personalized nutrition advice
- Exercise form correction
- Recovery optimization
- Injury prevention strategies

## Getting Started

1. Navigate to the **Chat** section in PocketGymAI
2. Tell the coach about your fitness journey
3. Ask specific questions or request full programs
4. The coach will reference research to back up recommendations

## Example Conversations

### Example 1: Complete Beginner

**You**: "Hi! I'm totally new to fitness. I want to build muscle but I don't know where to start."

**Coach**: *Asks for more details: age, weight, available equipment, schedule*

**You**: "I'm 25, weigh 75kg, have a basic home gym with dumbbells, and can train 3 times a week."

**Coach**: *Reviews research on beginner programming and generates a 3-day split*

Expected Response Topics:
- Progressive overload principle (increasing weight/reps weekly)
- Rep range for hypertrophy (8-15 reps)
- Rest periods (60-90 sec for accessories)
- Nutrition (1.6g protein per kg = 120g protein daily)
- Recovery (7-9 hours sleep)

### Example 2: Intermediate Lifter - Plateau

**You**: "I've been training for 2 years and hit a plateau. My lifts aren't going up anymore."

**Coach**: *Assesses training history and programming*

**You**: "I do the same 4-day split every week for 8 months. I do 8-10 sets per muscle per week."

**Coach**: *References periodization and deload research*

Expected Response Topics:
- Deload week recommendation (50% volume, every 4-6 weeks)
- Volume analysis (10-20 sets per muscle optimal, possibly under-trained)
- Periodization models (linear vs undulating)
- Exercise variation (prevent adaptation plateau)
- Technique review on main lifts

---

### Example 3: Fat Loss While Preserving Muscle

**You**: "I want to lose fat but keep my muscle. How aggressive should my caloric deficit be?"

**Coach**: *Gets details on current training, stats, and goals*

**You**: "I'm 90kg, train 4x/week heavy, and want to get to 80kg in 3 months."

**Coach**: *Calculates optimal deficit based on research*

Expected Response Topics:
- Deficit = 20-25% (not 30%+) to preserve muscle
- Protein needs increase on deficit: 2.2-3g per kg
- Training frequency should INCREASE during cut (more stimulus)
- Rate of loss: 0.5-1 lb/week = 1.5-3 lbs/month (3 month goal is aggressive)
- Cardio strategy: HIIT over steady-state for muscle preservation

### Example 4: Specific Exercise Form

**You**: "I have lower back pain when I deadlift. Should I stop doing them?"

**Coach**: *Investigates pain location and lifting pattern*

**You**: "Pain is mid-lower back, and I feel like I'm rounding my spine at the bottom."

**Coach**: *Provides form cues from injury prevention research*

Expected Response Topics:
- Hip hinge pattern (bend at hips, not spine)
- Neutral spine importance
- Adequate ankle mobility allows proper positioning
- Alternatives: trap bar deadlift, sumo deadlift (reduce strain)
- Post-workout core work: planks, dead bugs
- When to seek professional help (if pain persists)

**You**: "As a woman, can I build as much muscle as men? And does my cycle affect training?"

**Coach**: *References female-specific training research*

Expected Response Topics:
- Women can build muscle at similar RATE as men (per unit training)

### Example 6: Mobility & Injury Prevention

**You**: "I want to improve my squat depth. My knees cave inward during heavy sets."

**Coach**: *Assesses mobility and strength limiting factors*

You: "I'm quite stiff and my glutes feel weak."

**Coach**: *Provides mobility drills and activation work*

Expected Response Topics:
- Ankle dorsiflexion: daily stretches (2-3 min)
- Hip mobility: 90/90 stretch, deep squat holds (3-5 min daily)
- Glute activation: clams, glute bridges (before lifting)
- Cueing: "drive knees out", "proud chest"
- Box squats for strength development with better mechanics
- Single-leg work strengthens stabilizers

### Example 7: Supplement Recommendations

**You**: "Should I take supplements? What's worth the money?"

**Coach**: *Reviews supplement research*

Expected Response Topics:
- Creatine monohydrate: 5g daily, proven 5-15% strength gains, cheapest
- Protein powder: convenient if protein intake low, not magic
- Beta-alanine: 3-5g daily for high-rep work, minimal for strength
- Caffeine: 3-6mg/kg pre-workout, slight performance boost
- Vitamin D: if deficient (blood test), supports recovery
- Skip wasteful supplements: BCAA alone, testosterone boosters, etc.

### Example 8: Workout Split Selection

**You**: "PPL vs Upper/Lower? I have 5-6 days to train."

**Coach**: *Reviews both periodization models*

Expected Response Topics:
- Both effective with proper volume
- Push/Pull/Legs: 6 days/week, higher frequency per muscle (3-4x)
- Upper/Lower: 4 days/week, still frequency 2-3x, more time-efficient
- PPL pros: higher frequency allows better recovery distribution
- Upper/Lower pros: simpler programming, less time per session
- Recommendation: depends on recovery capacity and schedule preference

### Example 9: Training & Stress Management

**You**: "I work 60+ hours/week and feel exhausted. Training hard doesn't help—I'm getting weaker."

**Coach**: *Addresses overtraining from life stress*

Expected Response Topics:
- Overtraining Syndrome: high volume + intensity + inadequate recovery
- Stress is ADDITIVE (life stress = training stress)
- Solution: reduce training volume 30-50% temporarily
- Focus on sleep 7-9 hours (prioritize over training)
- Deload week: 50% volume for 1 week
- Re-assess after 2 weeks when recovered

---

### Example 10: Return to Training After 6 Months Off

**You**: "I took 6 months off from training. How should I restart?"

**Coach**: *Reviews detraining and retraining research*

Expected Response Topics:
- Muscle memory: much faster regain than initial gains
- Start at 50% previous loads, build back over 4 weeks
- Higher frequency initially: 3-4x/week (higher frequency = faster progress)
- Focus on form, not weight
- Will likely reach previous strength within 4-6 weeks
- Possible to exceed previous levels if programming improvement

## Common Questions & Typical Responses

### "Can I build muscle and lose fat at the same time?"

**Coach Response**:
- **Yes, but**: small caloric deficit (100-200 kcal), not aggressive
- **Only if**: beginner with surplus training stimulus
- **Better approach**: lean bulk (surplus) then cut (deficit) cycles
- **Research shows**: simultaneous gain/loss happens slowly, better to focus on one

### "How often should I do cardio?"

**Coach Response**:
- **General fitness**: 150 min/week moderate or 75 min/week vigorous
- **For muscle builders**: <5 hours/week steady-state (won't threaten gains)
- **Better for muscle**: HIIT 2-3x/week (20-30 min, time-efficient)
- **Combine with**: resistance training 2-3x/week

### "Should I work to failure?"

**Coach Response**:
- **No**: research shows 1-2 reps in reserve optimal
- **Why**: reduces injury risk, allows more volume
- **Exception**: final set of day for fatigue management
- **Recommendation**: RPE (Rate of Perceived Exertion) 6-8/10

### "What about meal timing?"

**Coach Response**:
- **Not critical**: total daily intake matters far more
- **General guideline**: 20-40g protein per meal
- **Pre-workout**: eat carbs 1-3 hours before (performance boost)
- **Post-workout**: within 2 hours is fine (non-essential)
- **Focus on**: total protein, calories, consistency

### "Can I train the same muscle daily?"

**Coach Response**:
- **Advanced only**: daily training requires specific programming
- **General rule**: 48 hours between heavy sessions for same muscle
- **Frequency**: 2-3x per week optimal for most
- **Exception**: different angles/rep ranges day-to-day possible

## Pro Tips for Getting Better Advice

1. **Be specific**: Instead of "How do I train?", say "I'm intermediate at squat/bench/deadlift, want hypertrophy, 5 days/week"

2. **Provide context**: Include weight, age, experience level, goals, schedule

3. **Ask progressively**: Start broad, then drill down into specific areas you need

4. **Challenge assumptions**: "Is that really research-backed?" Coach will cite sources

5. **Share feedback**: "I tried that program, my knees hurt" - helps coach adjust

6. **Break it down**: If too much info, ask for week 1 only, then week 2 later

---

## When to Seek Professional Help

The AI Coach is excellent for **general fitness guidance** but consult professionals for:

- **Sharp pain during exercise** → Physical therapist or sports doctor
- **Medical conditions** (diabetes, heart disease, etc.) → Doctor or registered dietitian
- **Detailed form analysis** → In-person coach or physical therapist
- **Mental health & disordered eating** → Licensed therapist or counselor
- **Advanced drug-assisted training** → Specific medical supervision

## FAQ

**Q: Can the coach create a custom plan just for me?**
A: Yes! Provide your stats, goals, equipment, and schedule. The coach synthesizes research into a personalized plan.

**Q: Does the advice change if I get stronger?**
A: Yes, progressively. Tell the coach your updated stats and needs—programming should evolve.

**Q: What if I disagree with the advice?**
A: Ask why! The coach cites research backing recommendations. You can negotiate based on preferences or constraints.

**Q: Is this better than a real coach?**
A: Different strengths. AI coach is 24/7, evidence-based, and personalized. Real coaches provide accountability and real-time form correction.
